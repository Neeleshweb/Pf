Last Updated: 21 July 2026  ·  Effective Date: 21 July 2026

Product Judgment Labs ("Product Judgment Labs," "we," "us," or "our") operates the Product Judgment Labs website, platform, and related services (collectively, the "Service").

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, retain, and protect information when you access or use the Service.

By using the Service, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy.

1. Who We Are

Product Judgment Labs is a platform designed to help product professionals practice, evaluate, and improve their product judgment through activities including:

  • Product decision cases;
  • Case submissions;
  • Peer responses and reviews;
  • Judgment exercises;
  • Performance scoring;
  • Rankings and leaderboards;
  • Personal performance analytics;
  • AI-assisted insights and diagnoses, where applicable; and
  • Related educational and professional-development experiences.

For privacy questions, requests, or concerns, contact:

  • Email: privacy@productjudgmentlabs.com
  • Website: https://www.productjudgmentlabs.com

2. Information We Collect

We follow a data-minimization approach and seek to collect only information reasonably necessary to operate and improve the Service.

You may create or access an account using Google Sign-In. When you choose to authenticate through Google, Google may provide us with information associated with your Google account, depending on the permissions requested and granted during authentication.

This may include:

  • Your name;
  • Email address;
  • Google account identifier;
  • Profile image;
  • Authentication information necessary to establish your account; and
  • Other information expressly disclosed to you during the Google authorization process.

We do not receive your Google password. We do not intentionally request access to your Google Drive, Gmail, contacts, Google Photos, or other Google services unless we separately disclose that access and request the relevant permission. The specific information we receive depends on the Google authentication scopes configured for Product Judgment Labs.

We use information to:
