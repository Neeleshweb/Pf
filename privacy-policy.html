Privacy Policy

Last updated: 18 July 2026

1. Who we are

Product Judgment Labs ("we", "the product") is operated by Neelesh Singh and hosted at productjudgmentlabs.com. It offers GutCheck, DecisionRoom and the Performance Center. You can reach us at Neelesh.Singh@dal.ca.

2. What we collect

  • Account information — when you sign in with Google, we receive your email address and basic profile information.
  • Your alias and profile — the display alias you choose, plus optional self-reported context such as role, years of experience, product type and industry.
  • Your activity — the cases you post, the responses you write, the GutCheck calls you make, and the scores and rankings derived from them.
  • Technical data — standard server and security logs kept by our hosting and database providers.

We do not collect payment card details, and we do not sell your data to anyone.

3. How we use it

  • To create and secure your account.
  • To run the product — showing your cases and responses to other members under your alias.
  • To calculate your scores, leaderboard position and Performance Vault insights.
  • To keep the service safe, including investigating reported or abusive content.

4. What other people can see

Your alias and any optional context you add are shown alongside your cases and responses. Your real name, email address and employer are never displayed to other members. Your Performance Vault is private to you; only aggregate rankings appear on public leaderboards.

5. Service providers

We rely on a small number of processors to run the service: Supabase (database and authentication), Google (sign-in), and Cloudflare (hosting and content delivery). Each processes data on our behalf under its own terms.

6. Cookies and local storage

We use browser local storage to keep you signed in and to remember interface preferences such as your chosen theme. We do not use advertising or cross-site tracking cookies.

7. Retention

We keep your account data for as long as your account exists. Cases and responses may remain visible under your alias after you leave, so that discussions other members relied on stay intact — tell us if you would like them removed.

8. Your rights

You can ask us to access, correct, export or delete your personal data. Email Neelesh.Singh@dal.ca and we will respond within a reasonable period. Depending on where you live, you may also have the right to complain to a data protection authority.

9. Children

This product is intended for working professionals and is not directed at anyone under 16.

10. Changes

If we make a material change to this policy we will update the date at the top of this page and, where appropriate, tell you in the product.

Please note: this document is a good-faith starting point written for a small, early-stage product — it is not legal advice. Before any wider public launch, have a qualified lawyer in your jurisdiction review and adapt it.
