Quick Reference
PM Frameworks Cheatsheet

The frameworks every PM should have memorized — with a one-line description of what each does and when to use it.

RICE
Reach · Impact · Confidence · Effort
Prioritization scoring model. Cuts through opinion-based backlog debates.
CIRCLES
PM Product Design Framework
Comprehend, Identify, Report, Cut, List, Evaluate, Summarize. Ace product sense interviews.
JTBD
Jobs To Be Done
Understand the real job users hire your product for. Surfaces motivations, not features.
OKR
Objectives & Key Results
Tie your product work to company goals. The bridge between strategy and execution.
HEART
Happiness·Engagement·Adoption·Retention·Task success
Google's user-centered metrics framework. Measure UX quality at scale.
ICE
Impact · Confidence · Ease
Lightweight prioritization for growth experiments. Fast, opinionated, useful.
5 WHYs
Root Cause Analysis
Ask "why" 5 times to reach the real problem behind any customer complaint or metric drop.
SWOT
Strengths·Weaknesses·Opportunities·Threats
Strategic overview of a product or company in the competitive landscape.
KANO
Kano Model
Classify features as Basic, Performance, or Delighters. Set expectations vs delight the right way.
STAR
Situation · Task · Action · Result
Behavioral interview storytelling format. Makes your experience sound like a highlight reel.
MoSCoW
Must / Should / Could / Won't
Simple scope management for sprint and release planning. Fast and clear for cross-functional teams.
HOOK
Nir Eyal's Habit Loop
Trigger → Action → Variable Reward → Investment. Build products that form habits, not dependencies.
